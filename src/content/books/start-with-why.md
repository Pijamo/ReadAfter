Simon Sinek's *Start with Why* is built on a deceptively simple idea: people don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it. Sinek introduces the Golden Circle — a framework of three concentric circles representing Why, How, and What — and argues that the most inspiring leaders and organizations in the world all think, act, and communicate from the inside out, starting with their purpose (Why) rather than their product (What).

The book draws its most powerful examples from Apple, the Wright Brothers, and Martin Luther King Jr. Apple doesn't just sell computers — it challenges the status quo and empowers individuals. The Wright Brothers didn't just want to build a flying machine — they believed that powered flight would change the world. King didn't just have a plan for civil rights — he had a dream. In each case, Sinek shows how starting with a clear sense of purpose created loyalty, innovation, and movements that transcended the transactional.

Sinek supports his framework with insights from biology — specifically, how the limbic brain, which governs feelings and decision-making, responds to "Why" messages, while the neocortex processes "What" information. This neurological grounding gives the Golden Circle a scientific basis beyond mere motivational rhetoric. The writing is accessible and repetitive by design, hammering the central idea until it becomes impossible to forget.
